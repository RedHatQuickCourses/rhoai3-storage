Making RHOAI Data Connectivity and Storage Work for You

The AI Factory: Automating for Velocity and Scale

You don’t build a car by hand. You shouldn’t build an AI platform by hand either.

In the early stages of AI adoption, organizations often treat every model as a unique "pet." Data Scientists submit a ticket for storage. Operations manually provisions a PVC. Security manually audits an S3 bucket. A developer hardcodes a connection string.

This manual approach works for one model. It collapses at ten.

The business value of automating Red Hat OpenShift AI (RHOAI) storage is not just about hiding passwordsâ€”it is about Standardization. It transforms your platform from a chaotic collection of research environments into a predictable AI Factory.

The Three Business Outcomes of Automation

By automating the "Plumbing" (Data Connections and Storage Classes), you deliver three specific outcomes that leadership cares about:

1. Acceleration (Time-to-Science)

The Problem: In manual environments, a new Data Scientist spends their first week fighting for access. They wait 2 days for a ticket to open a firewall port to S3. They wait 1 day for a Persistent Volume Claim (PVC) to be approved. The Automated Solution: You script standard Data Connections and Storage Classes. The Outcome: Onboarding takes minutes. A researcher joins a project, selects the pre-approved "Corporate Data Lake" connection, mounts the "Team Shared Storage," and starts training immediately.

2. Cost Efficiency (FinOps)

The Problem: "Shadow IT." Without governed storage options, users download massive datasets to expensive, unmanaged block storage (RWO) and leave them there forever. Or worse, they download terabytes of redundant data to local laptops. The Automated Solution: You provide a "Well-Lit Path": * Shared Storage (RWX): One 500GB volume shared by 10 users (saving 4.5TB of duplication). * Modelcars (OCI): Streaming models from a registry instead of downloading them to disk. The Outcome: You drastically reduce cloud storage spend and data egress fees by eliminating redundancy.

3. Production Stability (Environment Parity)

The Problem: "It works on my machine." A model works perfectly in the Dev environment because the data scientist manually tweaked the S3 path. It fails in Production because the path is different, and the manual tweak wasn’t documented. The Automated Solution: You abstract the environment using Data Connections. The code simply asks for conn-s3. * In Dev, your automation points conn-s3 to a "Sandbox Bucket." * In Prod, your automation points conn-s3 to the "Live Data Lake." The Outcome: The code never changes. You achieve 100% Environment Parity, significantly reducing deployment failures.

The Architecture of the Factory

In this lab, you will act as the Factory Architect. You will stop clicking buttons and start writing the code that defines your factory floor. You will automate three critical layers:

image::images/rhoai-layered-storage-architecture.png

Layer 1: The Warehouse (Data Connections)

  • What it is: The "Loading Dock" for external data.

  • The Automation: Instead of asking users to manage keys, you deploy standardized Connection Types (S3, OCI, URI).

  • Business Value: Ensures that if the security team rotates the keys, the business process doesn’t stop.

Layer 2: The Workbench (Cluster Storage)

  • What it is: The "Assembly Line" where work happens.

  • The Automation: You define Storage Classes that map to business needs.

  • "Scratchpad" (RWO): Fast, temporary, personal storage.

  • "Collaboration" (RWX): Shared project storage for team-based iteration.

  • Business Value: Prevents data silos. If a team member leaves, their work remains in the shared project volume, not locked on a personal drive.

Layer 3: The Supply Chain (Modelcars & URI)

  • What it is: The delivery mechanism for model assets.

  • The Automation: You treat models like software artifacts using OCI Registries.

  • Business Value: Faster rollouts. A new model version is just a new container tag, instantly available to all production services.

Your Mission: Script the Setup

In this lab, you will use the OpenShift CLI (oc) to build this factory. You will verify that your scripts can take a blank project and turn it into a fully connected, storage-ready Data Science environment in under 60 seconds.
